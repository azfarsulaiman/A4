Proposition: Globalization 2.0 (starting 2000) has been far more adverse for the Global South than the Global North.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Choice of Colors (1): I strived to use red for my visualizations prominently in my visualization to arrest audience's attention to the worsening sitation for the Global South. The color red is able to generate that effect, which is why its used in harmful or warning signs and labels, and usually has negative undertones. I wanted to direct the audience to the negative consequences os performacne on social indicators such as fertility rates and rise of secondary income vs primary income, which may altogether not be societally bad.
  • Drop Effect (2): Another aspect to convey the worsening of the situation was through the drop effect, complementing the "trickle-down" label tag. The bar graphs are encoded accordingly to dmeonstrate this drop trend, across countries and years. I sorted the information such as for the dendogram or the Income differntial graph to convvey this effect strongly. This works for the Income and Fertility rates graphs and is also able to confuse the reader on the refugees graphs, for which even though the share for every subsequent country is dramatically reduced, it still inhibits a feeling of loss.
  • Power-packed abels (1.5): To enhance the loss effect for the Global South and used the labels strategically to convey this point. Use of words such as "Trickle Down", "Decreasing", War on Terror" and "Informal" are peppered on my visualizations to pack a sense of emotion and not only supplement the designs but also direect the reader accordingly. Even though War on Terror has a bigger share on creating refugees in Global South, I juxtaposed this effect with the overall worsening situation for the Global South with the rise of Globalization.
